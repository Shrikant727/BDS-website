Enrique Naredo

Postdoctoral Researcher

Enrique Naredo is a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Biocomputing and Developmental Systems (BDS) research group at the University of Limerick (UL), which is part of Lero. He is an expert in Grammatical Evolution (GE) commonly used powerful automatic programming technique proudly invented by BDS Group, which is used in over 4,000 scientific papers and referenced in ten patents. Enrique is the senior postdoctoral researcher on the Automatic Design of Digital Circuits (ADDC) project, a 1.8 million euro project with partners including Intel, S3 and Emdahlo. He supervises PhD students within the BDS as well as MSc students enrolled in the Ireland’s First Industry-Driven Masters in Artificial Intelligence at UL. Enrique has authored 24 conference and journal papers.

Meghana Kshirsagar

Postdoctoral Researcher
Meghana Kshirsagar is a Postdoctoral Researcher and data scientist at Lero, the Science Foundation Ireland Research Centre for Software and is affiliated with the Biocomputing and Developmental Systems Research Group hosted within the department of Computer Science and Information Systems at University of Limerick, Ireland.She has Bachelors degree from Government Engineering College,Aurangabad, India , Masters degree from Patiala University, India and PhD from Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University,India.All her degrees are in Computer Science and Engineering. She is currently working on a Science Foundation Ireland funded project (€1.8M) in collaboration with Intel to automatically design digital circuits. Her current research also focuses on designing machine learning,deep learning and data mining models for smart transportation as well as developing blockchain assisted infrastructures for smart healthcare and plastic waste management.She has applied grammatical evolution tools to design time series forecasting models. During her PhD thesis which was sponsored under world bank project,her research involved the broader domain of neuro-cognition where she designed machine learning models to analyse connectivity strengths in populations afflicted with mental illness such as Schizophrenia, Alzheimer, Dementia while performing different tasks and used fMRI images in her study.A part of her PhD thesis also focused on designing machine learning models for classification of brain tumors from real world MRS images collected from various hospitals and diagnostic centres. She has authored close to 60 scientific publications which includes international conferences,book chapters and journal articles.She has chaired many sessions in machine learning,artificial intelligence and evolutionary computation conferences.She has also served on technical programme committee in many IEEE conferences and has organized special sessions in Springer sponsored machine learning conferences.She is member of many scientific committees and serves on editorial board as review editor in the Frontiers in Blockchain journal. She has supervised 40 graduate students, and taught numerous basic and advanced courses in data mining and algorithms to both undergraduates and graduates during her tenure as Assistant and Associate Professor in computer science department at Government Engineering College, Aurangabad from 2002-2019.She represented her department in academic councils that finalised strategic decisions for the department .She also served in many of departments core committees that oversaw successful accreditation (thrice during her tenure),quality assurance ,syllabus design and various interview panels for graduates admission process and appointment of adhoc faculties. She was involved in curriculum design for many undergrad and graduate courses such as data mining and warehousing ,data science and fundamental and advanced algorithms. She was also editor-in-chief for the institute's annual magazine “ Catalyst from 2015-2019
She has successfully acquired funds to the tune of €10,000 from world bank project for the development of departments staff and students She was successful in securing funds to the tune of €5,000 for organisation of workshops for student empowerment.She collaborated on various industrial sponsored projects that resulted in successful MOU’s and created hiring opportunities amongst departments students. Her mentoring to teams participating in Smart India Hackathon (SIH-2019) has lead to many successes.Simultaneously she has mentored in EU-Hackathon(2020) and hosted a workshop session at Vizathon(Visualization Hackathon, Coronavirus Visualization Team,July 2021) where she was also invited as judge.She has organised and is co chairing “Evathon”(The Evolutionary Computation Hackathon,Aug 2021).She has collaborated on 20 articles on medium (perspectivesondatascience) as an initiative to facilitate learnings in data science community.
She believes strongly in women empowerment and all her activities have an important component around it. She has delivered national level talks in her home country India to present success stories in Gender Equality,Diversity and Inclusiveness.She loves to explore different parts of the world and has so far visited 9 European Countries ,the UAE and South Asian countries of Singapore and Thailand.During her free time she loves to read Indian mythological and philosophical books and English literary classics and tunes in to Hindi as well as English music as stress busters.

Gauri Vaidya

PhD student
Gauri Vaidya is a first-year Ph.D. student at Biocomputing and Developmental System Group (BDS) in the Computer Science and Information Systems (CSIS) department at the University of Limerick, where she is supervised by Professor Conor Ryan, Full Professor at the University of Limerick. Her research is in the field of Evolutionary Computation which is a subfield of Artificial Intelligence. During her research, she will develop techniques to develop a multimodal Grammatical Evolution tool that represents multiple different data modalities and aims at integrating them to get valuable insights.
Gauri holds a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the Government College of Engineering, Aurangabad, India. Her Bachelor thesis was to develop a blockchain-based First Information Report (FIR) for the City Police Service to avoid malicious tampering.
